## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs an in-depth **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a telecom customer churn dataset to understand why customers leave the company and identify the factors that influence customer retention.

Using Python and data visualization techniques, the analysis uncovers patterns related to customer demographics, subscription plans, payment methods, contract types, tenure, and additional services. The project concludes with business insights and recommendations to help improve customer retention.

## 📈 Key Insights

- Nearly **1 in 4 customers** have churned, indicating a significant opportunity to improve retention.
- Customers with **month-to-month contracts** show the highest churn rates compared to long-term contracts.
- **Senior citizens** are more likely to leave than younger customers.
- Customers without **Online Security** and **Tech Support** services tend to churn more frequently.
- Customers paying through **Electronic Check** have the highest churn rate.
- Customers who stay longer with the company are much less likely to churn.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Encourage customers to switch from month-to-month plans to long-term contracts through loyalty incentives.
- Promote value-added services such as Online Security and Tech Support to improve customer retention.
- Launch targeted retention campaigns for senior citizens and newly acquired customers.
- Offer discounts for customers using high-risk payment methods to encourage automatic payment options.
- Build personalized retention strategies for customers showing early signs of churn.
